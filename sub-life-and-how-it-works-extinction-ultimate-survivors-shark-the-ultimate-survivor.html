Sharks appeared about 420 million years ago and are among some of the oldest vertebrates living on Earth today. The design of the shark has remained largely unchanged and they were so successful, they managed to outlast other big prehistoric marine predators like ichthyosaurs and pliosaurs.

Unfortunately, a shark's skeleton is made of cartilage which is soft compared to bone and does not preserve well as a fossil. However, their teeth are tough and have been found in the thousands. Based on tooth size and shape, experts have been able to estimate their size and diet.

The most famous of prehistoric sharks is the Megalodon, meaning 'giant tooth'. This big bruiser appeared soon after the dinosaur extinction. Megalodon was a vicious predator, a king of the ocean measuring 20 metres long and weighing over 50 tonnes! That's more than five times the size of today's great white shark!

Vertebrates

Vertebrates are animals that have a backbone or spinal column. Mammals, reptiles and birds are all vertebrates.
