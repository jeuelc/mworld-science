The Creation of O2

 
click here click here

Take a deep breath. Let it fill your lungs. Did you know that you're breathing in oxygen gas (O2) that was created by bacteria 3.5 billion years ago?

When life first began, there was no O2 in the atmosphere. Eventually, one group of micro-organisms called 'cyanobacteria' evolved and started to undergo a process called 'photosynthesis'. Photosynthesis helped cyanobacteria use energy from the sun to grow and also caused the release of O2 into the atmosphere. Photosynthesis is still used by plants today in the same way by using the suns light, water and carbon dioxide (CO2) to produce O2.

But O2 can be poisonous and, ironically, it nearly wiped out all life before it began! About 2.5 billion years ago, most simple life was happy to grow without O2; that is, until cyanobacteria came along and polluted the atmosphere for the first time with O2!
