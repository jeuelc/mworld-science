Genetic Engineering

 
click here click here

Never before has the direct manipulation of life and evolution been possible until the dawn of modern humans. Early in our history we manipulated evolution by selectively breeding animals and plants for certain traits tailored to human needs. However, only since the 1970s have humans been able to directly manipulate an organism's DNA.

The process is complex but basically, by injecting new pieces of DNA into a cell, we can change an organism in extraordinary ways. We've engineered plants to be bigger and tastier and to resist herbicides; we've even made fish 'glow' with fluorescent colours! Even more astonishing is that in 2010, 'Synthia', a bacterium, became the first form of artificial life created by genetic engineering!

The applications are endless and may help us cure diseases, make direct copies of ourselves or even bring extinct monsters of the past back to life!
