Mutants

DNA is like a blueprint for a machine that carries all the information needed to create a fully functioning organism. In the process of creating new cells, DNA copies itself and the information it holds for the new cell. This doesn't always go exactly to plan though! On the odd occasion, during the copying process, DNA makes an error. These errors, called mutations, can vary in their effects. They can range from being as harmless as a skin blemish to the extremes of disease and physical abnormality.

In an evolutionary sense though, changes are often subtle, random and often take millions of years of tiny steps for one organism to evolve into another. For example, today's birds are the descendants of dinosaurs that started growing feathers for warmth and display. Eventually their feathered arms evolved to be stronger, which in turn assisted the first stages of flight!
