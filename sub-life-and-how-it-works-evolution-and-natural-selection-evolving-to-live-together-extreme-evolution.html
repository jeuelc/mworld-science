Evolution can create some mind-blowing interactions between organisms and their habitats... a bird simply 'disappearing', a trickster plant that looks like a bee and a frog that can survive turning into an ice block... literally!

The tawny frogmouth bird of Australia loves to eat small insects and lizards but can also be dinner itself. To avoid detection it 'disappears', thanks to being a nearly identical colour to the trees it lives in and its ability to turn stiff to look like a tree stump!

Orchids are a plant thats not only beautiful but very cunning! The bee orchid has petals which look like a female bumblebee and this tricks male bumblebees into landing on the petals and collecting pollen to fertilise other orchids!

And the wood frog of North America can turn into a frogsicle and survive! The wood frog has evolved anti-freeze blood which protects its organs during the deep freeze of winter.
