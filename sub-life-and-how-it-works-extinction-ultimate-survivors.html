Despite extinctions wiping out the majority life, there have been some organisms that have survived. Some of these ultimate survivors even still look the same as they did tens of millions of years ago!

But what makes an ultimate survivor? There are several possible reasons why some survive and some do not; plants in general are hardier than animals and their seeds can survive long times without sprouting. This is especially useful in extreme conditions like droughts and freeze-overs.

Large animals tend to die out because they need more food than smaller animals. Also, if an organism's population is spread far and wide, it increases the chance that one place may be better for survival than another.

Fast reproductive rates also help as this increases the chance to adapt over generations to a changing environment.
