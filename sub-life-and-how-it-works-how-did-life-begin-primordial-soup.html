Primordial Soup!

The early Earth's atmosphere was made up of molecules which were combinations of carbon (C), hydrogen (H), nitrogen (N) and oxygen (O).

The atmosphere was extremely hot, had no protection from the sun's UV rays and had constant electrical storms far worse than anything we've ever seen! Eventually, the energy from UV rays and the electricity allowed the molecules to react and form new, organic molecules such as the amino acids.

Over millions of years, the Earth began to cool and water vapour condensed into rain, carrying the organic molecules with it. The rain began filling up 'bowls' on Earth, creating warm 'soups'. These primordial (really, really old!) soups became the first oceans and lakes where the amino acids accumulated. They continued to mix until they formed chains of proteins, the building blocks of life. Everything still continued to mix and the chains soon linked to form the first cells!

UV rays

UV stands for Ultra Violet. UV rays are part of the energy that comes from the sun but they're invisible to human eyes! See Light in Wonders of Science to find out more about UV rays.
