The Rise of the Mammals

Mammals appeared on Earth about the same time as the dinosaurs (225 million years ago) but unlike the dinosaurs, they evolved slowly and were mainly small and rodent-like. It wasn't until the end of the dinosaurs 65 million years ago that mammals were free to evolve and flourish to become the dominant life form on Earth.

The key to the survival of mammals after the meteor impact is still a mystery but it may be because of their lifestyle. Small mammals could retreat to burrows which offered protection against the soaring temperatures following the impact. They also had a flexible diet so they could scavenge and live off whatever was about, including dead dinosaurs.

The mass extinction trimmed back the tree of life and allowed the dawn of a new dominant species, the mammals. Without the end of the dinosaurs, you would not exist!
