The world is facing unprecedented change; the climate is warming faster than ever before and the ever-increasing population of humans is placing incredible stress on natural ecosystems. Scientists estimate that species are becoming extinct 1000 to 10,000 times faster than naturally occurs!

But all is not lost! Conservation efforts have resulted in wildlife reserves covering 10% of the entire planet, helping to restore, protect and maintain entire ecosystems. Some amazing examples include the national parks of the world such as Kruger in Africa, the Great Barrier Reef in Australia and Yellowstone in North America.

Conservation is a powerful tool. In relatively short periods, we've helped animals rebound from the brink of extinction. In just over 40 years, we've brought the humpback whale from 10% to about 35% of its original population. American bison numbers were only 750 a little over 110 years ago and are now at 350,000!
