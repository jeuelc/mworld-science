The Five Extinctions

In Earth's history the five big extinction events are known as the mass extinctions of the Ordovician-Silurian (443 million years ago), the Late Devonian (359 million years ago), the Permian (248 million years ago), the Triassic-Jurassic (200 million years ago), and the most famous, the dinosaur-ending extinction of the Cretaceous Tertiary extinction event (65 million years ago).The most catastrophic of all these extinctions, though, was the Permian. An estimated 96% of all life became extinct! Its unclear what single event caused any of the extinctions but theories include abrupt climate change, mass volcanic activity or even a collision with a meteor or comet. The best example of what is currently believed to have caused a particular extinction is that of the dinosaurs, when 65 million years ago, a meteor slammed into the Yucatan Peninsula in Mexico, bringing the end of one world and the beginning of another.
